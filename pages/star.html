Fun Facts

Inception

Originally created in the UK under the name "Opal Fruits" in 1960. The candy was introduced in the United States in 1967, and its name was changed to Starburst to reflect its burst of flavor. The original flavors of Opal Fruits were strawberry, lemon, orange, and lime

Ingredients

Starburst candy originally contained gelatin, but the company switched to a vegan recipe using rice starch and pectin in 2020. The new recipe still maintains the fruity flavors and chewy texture that Starburst is known for, making it suitable for those following a vegan diet.

Taste

The debate over the yellow Starburst's flavor has become so widespread that the company has even released limited edition "all pink" and "all red" packs due to their popularity. The yellow Starburst has been a topic of debate among fans for years, with some people loving its lemon flavor and others finding it too tart.

Bonus

"Sweet Heat" was released. It featured a combination of sweet and spicy flavors, with flavors such as Fiery Watermelon, Flamin' Orange, Strawberry Mango, and Pipin' Pineapple. The flavor was so popular that it has since been released as a permanent addition to the Starburst lineup.
